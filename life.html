INTRODUCTION

Each god descends from his or her own genealogy, pursues differing interests, has a certain area of expertise, and is governed by a unique personality; however, these descriptions arise from a multiplicity of archaic local variants, which do not always agree with one another. When these gods are called upon in poetry, prayer, or cult, they are referred to by a combination of their name and epithets, that identify them by these distinctions from other manifestations of themselves

ZEUS
Known as the king of the gods and ruler of Mount Olympus, Zeus is the god of the sky, lightning, thunder, law, order, and justice. He is often depicted with a thunderbolt and eagle.

HERA
Zeus's sister and wife, Hera is the queen of the gods and goddess of marriage, women, childbirth, and family. She is known for her jealous and vengeful nature, particularly against Zeus's numerous affairs.

POSEIDON
Zeus's brother, Poseidon is the god of the sea, earthquakes, and horses. He wields a trident and is often depicted with sea creatures. He is known for his tempestuous personality and rivalry with Zeus.

ATHENA
Born fully grown and armored from Zeus's forehead, Athena is the goddess of wisdom, warfare, strategy, and handicrafts. She is a patron of heroes and is often depicted with an owl and aegis (shield) and spear.
